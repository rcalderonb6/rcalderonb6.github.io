Rodrigo Calderón

Central European Institute of Cosmology · Na Slovance 1999/2, 182 21, Prague, Czech Republic · calderon@fzu.cz

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in the Cosmology group at KASI. My research focuses on the phenomenology of models beyond the standard $\Lambda\rm CDM$ paradigm. I am particularly interested in developping and applying advanced statistical methods to Large Scale Structure data from ongoing and upcoming surveys such as DESI, Euclid & LSST, with the main goal of constraining the properties of dark sector and modified theories of Gravity.

Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection between theory and observations. I primarily focus on testing the underlying pillars of the standard model of Cosmology using various observational probes. I am particularly interested in the use of advanced statistical techniques to test the consistency of the current measurements, and/or to look for possible hints of new physics. Some of the questions my research tries to address include: Is the Universe statistically isotropic, as encoded in the cosmological principle? Is dark energy in the form of a cosmological constant ($\Lambda$), or does it evolve with time? Is general relativity the correct theory of gravity at cosmological scales? What was the microscopic mechanism driving the early stage of accelerated expansion of the Universe (inflation)?

I am an active member of the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) and Vera C. Rubin (former LSST) Dark Energy Science (DESC) Collaborations. I am also involved in the Einstein Telescope Collaboration.
