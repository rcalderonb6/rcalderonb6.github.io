About me

I was born and raised in El Salvador, where I spent most of my early years before moving to Montpellier, France in 2013 to study Physics. I received my BSc. in Fundamental Physics in 2016 and MSc. in Particle Physics and Cosmology in 2018. My PhD thesis focused on the phenomenology of several extensions of the ΛCDM model, under the supervision of Prof. David Polarksi.

When I am not doing research, I spend most of time outdoors, or playing/listening to music. I also enjoy cooking and spending time with friends and family.

Scientific Interests

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Central European Institute of Cosmology (CEICO), Institute of Physics of the Czech Academy of Science. My research interests lie at the intersection between theory and observations, with a particular interest in the development and applications of advanced statistical methods, with the primary goal of testing the main assumptions underpinning modern Cosmology.

I am an active member of the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) and Vera Rubin (former LSST) Dark Energy Science (DESC) Collaborations. I am mostly involved in the analysis of large scale structure data, and constraining the properties of Dark Energy and modified theories of gravity through various different observations.
